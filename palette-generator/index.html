About Color Harmonies

Complementary uses colors opposite on the color wheel: high contrast, bold designs. Analogous uses neighboring colors for a cohesive, serene look.

Triadic spaces three colors equally around the wheel: vibrant yet balanced. Split-complementary offers contrast with less tension. Tetradic uses four colors in a square pattern. Monochromatic varies lightness of a single hue.
